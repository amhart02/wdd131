Content

Home page

Shrek (2001): Family & Children, an animated comedy that follows an ogre named Shrek who embarks on a quest to rescue Princess Fiona from a dragon-guarded castle. Along the way, he befriends a talkative donkey and learns important lessons about friendship and self-acceptance.

Ratatouille (2007): Family & Children, an animated comedy-drama from Pixar Animation Studios. It tells the story of Remy, a rat with a refined palate and dreams of becoming a chef. With the help of Linguini, a young garbage boy, Remy navigates the culinary world of Paris, showcasing his talent through cooking.

Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs (2009): Family & Children, an animated comedy based on the children's book by Judi and Ron Barrett. It follows Flint Lockwood, a young inventor who creates a machine that turns water into food. Chaos ensues as the machine goes haywire, causing oversized food to rain down on the town of Swallow Falls.

Inception (2010): Science Fiction, a science fiction thriller about a thief who enters the dreams of others to steal their secrets. He is tasked with planting an idea into the mind of a CEO through a complex process of shared dreaming and manipulation of reality.

Flipped (2010): Romantic Comedy, a romantic comedy-drama that follows two childhood neighbors, Juli and Bryce, who have opposite reactions to their evolving feelings for each other over the years. The story is told from both perspectives as they navigate friendship, family dynamics, and first love.

The Hunger Games (2012): Dystopian, a dystopian future where teenagers are selected to participate in a televised fight to the death as punishment for a past rebellion. Katniss Everdeen volunteers to take her sister's place, becoming a symbol of resistance against the oppressive Capitol.

Back to the Future (1985): Action-Adventure, a classic science fiction adventure comedy. It follows Marty McFly, who accidentally travels back in time to 1955 in a time-traveling DeLorean invented by his eccentric friend, Doc Brown. Marty must ensure his parents fall in love while finding a way to return to 1985.

Spider-Man (2002): Action-Adventure, a superhero film based on the Marvel Comics character. It follows Peter Parker, a high school student who gains spider-like abilities after being bitten by a genetically modified spider. He uses his newfound powers to fight crime as Spider-Man while balancing his personal life.

Napoleon Dynamite (2004): Comedy, a comedy film set in small-town Idaho. It follows the socially awkward and eccentric Napoleon Dynamite, a high school student who navigates his mundane life, quirky family dynamics, and attempts to help his new friend Pedro win the class presidency. The film became a cult classic known for its offbeat humor and memorable characters.

Hot Rod (2007): Comedy, a comedy film that follows Rod Kimble, a self-proclaimed stuntman who dreams of emulating his deceased father's legacy by performing a dangerous stunt to raise money for his stepfather's heart surgery. With the help of his friends, Rod attempts increasingly ridiculous stunts while dealing with personal challenges and comedic mishaps. The film blends slapstick humor with heartfelt moments and became known for its absurd and over-the-top comedic style.

Star Wars: Episode III - Revenge of the Sith (2005): Science Fiction, the third installment of the prequel trilogy in the Star Wars saga. It depicts the fall of Jedi Knight Anakin Skywalker to the dark side of the Force, his transformation into Darth Vader, and the rise of the Galactic Empire led by Emperor Palpatine.
